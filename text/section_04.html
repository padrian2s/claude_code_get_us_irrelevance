Explained Simply

In Simple Terms

Dario is painting a picture of two enormous changes happening at once. First: diseases that kill millions of people every year — cancer, heart disease, Alzheimer's — could largely be defeated. Second: the economy could grow at speeds never seen before. Normal GDP growth is about 2-3% per year. He's talking about 5-15% per year. That would mean the economy doubles in 5-7 years instead of the usual 25-35.

GDP Growth: GDP (Gross Domestic Product) is the total value of everything a country produces. Growth of 2-3% per year is considered healthy. Growth of 5-15% would be like the entire Industrial Revolution compressed into a few years. China's famous "economic miracle" peaked at about 14% growth — Dario is saying AI could deliver that to already-developed countries.
The catch: Even if the pie grows enormously, that doesn't mean everyone gets a bigger slice. Dario flags this himself: "Growth is really easy. It's the distribution that's hard." In other words, AI might make the world much richer overall, but if all that wealth goes to the people who own the AI, most people could still be worse off.

The Budget Trick

Here's a detail that sounds dry but is actually wild: if GDP grows that fast, tax revenue goes up massively too. The US deficit — which politicians fight about constantly — could basically solve itself without anyone doing anything, just because there's so much more economic activity being taxed. It's not that deficits don't matter; it's that AI-driven growth might make them irrelevant.

Why This Matters to You

If even half of this comes true, we're looking at a world where the biggest political question isn't "how do we grow the economy?" (the usual question) but "how do we share the growth?" That's a fundamentally different problem, and our political systems aren't designed for it.
