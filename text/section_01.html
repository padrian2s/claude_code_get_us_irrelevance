Explained Simply

What's Happening Here

This is the opening of an interview on the New York Times show "Interesting Times," hosted by Ross Douthat. His guest is Dario Amodei, the CEO of Anthropic — the company that makes Claude (the AI you might already be using). Anthropic is worth about $350 billion, making it one of the most valuable private companies in the world.

Anthropic: An AI safety company founded in 2021 by Dario Amodei and his sister Daniela, along with other former OpenAI researchers. They build Claude, a family of AI models. Their stated mission is to build AI that is safe, beneficial, and understandable.

Why This Interview Matters

Dario wrote two major essays: "Machines of Loving Grace" (the optimistic vision) and "The Adolescence of Technology" (the dangers). Ross is going to walk through both. The interview covers everything from curing cancer to job loss to whether AI might go rogue. Dario is unusual among tech CEOs because he openly talks about both the incredible promise AND the serious dangers of what he's building.

The Central Question

Ross frames the whole interview around one question: "Are the lords of artificial intelligence on the side of the human race?" In other words — the people building the most powerful technology in history, can we trust them? Are they thinking about us? This question hangs over everything that follows.
