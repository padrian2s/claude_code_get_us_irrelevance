Explained Simply

The Revenue Curve

Anthropic's revenue trajectory deserves a moment of pure awe: zero to $100 million in 2023, $1 billion in 2024, $9-10 billion in 2025, and adding billions more in January 2026 alone. That's 10x growth every year — a pace that, if it continued, would put Anthropic at $100 billion revenue by end of 2026 and $1 trillion by 2027. For context, Apple took 40 years to reach $100 billion in annual revenue. Anthropic might do it in four.

The two exponentials:
Exponential #1 — Capability: How smart the models are. This is moving fast and predictably, driven by scaling.
Exponential #2 — Diffusion: How fast the real economy adopts and benefits from those models. Also fast, but slower than capability.

The gap between these two exponentials is where all the confusion lives. Capability is already extraordinary. Adoption is still catching up. That's why people who work at AI labs seem to live in a different reality than everyone else — they're riding Exponential #1, while the rest of the world is on Exponential #2.

Is Diffusion Just Cope?

Dwarkesh pushes hard on this: if AI is really so useful, why does it need time to diffuse? Humans are way harder to hire — they need interviews, onboarding, they might not work out, they need salaries. AI should be easier. Dario's response is honest: even something as simple as Claude Code, which any individual developer can set up in minutes, takes months at a large enterprise. Not because the technology isn't ready, but because enterprises have legal teams, compliance requirements, procurement processes, and leaders who need convincing. These aren't irrational barriers — they exist for good reasons — but they're real friction.

Why "we would know it" matters: Dario explicitly says we do NOT currently have a "country of geniuses in a data center." This is an important reality check. Despite all the hype, the current models are not yet at the level where their impact is undeniable to everyone in a position of power. When that moment arrives — and Dario thinks it's 1-3 years away — it won't be subtle. It won't require benchmarks or debates. It will be obvious. We're not there yet. But the exponential is relentless.
"We're doing everything we can to make Anthropic's revenue grow 20 or 30x a year instead of 10x a year."

Read that again. They're trying to grow FASTER than 10x per year. The fact that "only" 10x annual growth is considered the floor — and they're pushing for 20-30x — tells you everything about how these companies see the moment. This isn't a normal business. It's a race where the prize is the most transformative technology in human history, and the penalty for losing is irrelevance.
